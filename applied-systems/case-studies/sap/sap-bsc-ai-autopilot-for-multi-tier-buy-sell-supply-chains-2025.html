Challenges


OEMs often purchase components directly from suppliers to ensure quality control but face major coordination issues when drop-shipping them to contract manufacturers. Without real-time visibility and automation, this process leads to delays, misaligned inventory, overstocking, and costly manual follow-ups—undermining the very efficiency and control the OEMs aim to achieve.

  • Visibility Gaps: OEMs and contract manufacturers struggle to gain real-time insights into supplier stock levels, leading to stockouts or over-ordering.

  • Manual, Inefficient Processes: Collaboration between OEMs, suppliers, and manufacturers is often slow and disjointed, relying on email chains and manual data exchanges.

  • Inaccurate Forecasting: Procurement teams lack AI-driven insights, leading to inaccurate forecasts and inefficient inventory planning.

  • Inefficient Drop-Shipping: Poor coordination leads to costly overstocking and delays.

  • Supply Chain Risks: ESG, geopolitical, and supplier performance risks go undetected, impacting costs and timelines.

Target Users

💛 OEM Buyers (e.g. Apple, Google, Tesla): Procurement teams managing component purchases from suppliers and product orders from contract manufacturers. Gain real-time insights into supplier stock, automate purchasing, and reduce procurement costs.

💙 Contract Manufacturers (e.g. Foxconn, Pegatron, Wistron ): Responsible for assembling and delivering finished products back to the OEM. Streamline collaboration with OEMs and Tier 2/3 suppliers, reducing line stoppages with real-time inventory visibility.

💚 Component Suppliers (e.g. Samsung, TSMC, Sony): Suppliers of raw materials and components used in production. Improve order accuracy, reduce excess inventory, and benefit from dynamic pricing and automated payments.

💜 Supply Chain Executives (SAP – Big Chance): Oversee performance, cost efficiency, and procurement risks. Leverage real-time insights and automated risk mitigation to enhance supply chain resilience.

Solution

6 AI Agents

SmartSupply AI introduces a suite of AI-powered agents that streamline and automate the entire Buy-Sell collaboration process, ensuring real-time transparency, smarter procurement, and faster decision-making.


Forecasting Agent

✅ Leverages historical data and real-time signals to predict demand.

✅ Enhances procurement planning accuracy.

✅ Prevents over-ordering and reduces stockouts.


Inventory Agent

✅ Provides real-time visibility into stock levels across all tiers.

✅ Sends automated alerts for potential shortages or excess inventory.


 Procurement Agent

✅ Automates purchase decisions based on demand forecasts and stock insights.

✅ Optimizes order cycles, reducing manual interventions.

✅ Auto-pays suppliers upon IoT-verified delivery, reducing payment delays.


Drop-Ship Optimizer Agent

✅ Automatically routes supplier-to-manufacturer orders, bypassing intermediate warehouses.

✅ Ensures direct shipping to reduce delays and costs.

 

Pricing & Risk Agent

✅ Monitors market conditions and supplier performance.

✅ Dynamically adjusts pricing and flags supply chain risks.

✅ Flags ESG, geopolitical, and supplier risks in real-time.


Multi-Tier Visibility Agent

✅ Offers a centralized dashboard with live insights into procurement, inventory, and risks.

✅ Improves transparency and collaboration between suppliers and manufacturers.

Summary

As a senior designer and strategist, I see this project as a game-changing solution tailored for high-revenue OEMs taking greater control of their supply chains. By addressing the unique challenges of direct component sourcing—such as logistical complexity, quality accountability, and financial risk—our AI-powered platform provides intelligent agents for forecasting, inventory visibility, procurement automation, and risk management. This strategic approach not only enhances operational efficiency but also strengthens supplier relationships and resilience, making it especially impactful for global leaders like Apple, Google, and Tesla navigating increasingly complex supply ecosystems.

Key Learnings and Future Improvements

  • Strategic Control Requires Intelligent Support - Direct sourcing gives OEMs more control, but it also demands advanced tools to manage increased complexity—AI agents fill this gap efficiently.

  • Visibility Across Tiers is Critical - Multi-tier inventory and procurement visibility is essential for preventing delays, avoiding overstock, and enabling faster decision-making.

  • Automation Drives Resilience - Automating procurement, forecasting, and drop-ship logistics reduces human error, cuts response times, and builds a more resilient supply chain ecosystem.

  • AI Changes UX Daily Work Type - Designers now focus less on traditional design work and more on studying users, crafting strategies, and serving them as personal assistants—making AI an integral part of the user experience.
